About Us

Saram (사람)

"Saram" in Korean is "사람", which means "person"

Saram in the World is a group of people who collectively seek to rediscover their humanity in an increasingly technological world.
Rearranging the characters within "사람" makes "삶", which means "life." As emerging technologies such as AI ask us to confront what it means to be human and to protect the well-being of humanity, we hope this think space can bring together different voices from different cultures, especially those less represented and marginalized in the status quo, in addressing these questions.

Origin

You Jeen Ha, Founder

After completing her Fulbright fellowship in Korea, You Jeen decided to work on making a think space for cross-national discussions surrounding AI ethics and policy. She envisions this platform to be a kind of Habermasian public sphere, calling for the involvement and participation of all relevant stakeholders.

our inspiration

Moon Jae In

19th President of South Korea (2017-2022)

Formerly a civil rights lawyer, Moon Jae In campaigned for president with the message '사람이 먼저다' (Humanity First). He not only served as a beacon of optimism and humanistic values for the Korean people and the world, but also actively worked to implement such values in his policies, including his approach to 4IR and AI. By encouraging a multistakeholder policymaking model, the Moon administration led the design and implementation of people-centered national AI ethics guidelines and created a multidisciplinary platform for continued discussion and debate. This think space would like to follow in his footsteps.
This think space is not proclaiming a particular political affiliation. It is simply respecting the accomplishments of a particular leader who helped implement a particular policymaking platform.
